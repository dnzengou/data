FINDING CO-FOUNDER(S): WHAT TO THINK ABOUT
Having a strong co-founding team is an important success factor for startups. Having two or more full-time co-founders on a team (rather than flying solo) significantly increases a startup‚Äôs likelihood of success. In any founding team, fully committed co-founders are greater than the sum of their individual parts; we strongly believe that teamwork makes the dream work.

Yet, many founders struggle to accept that they don‚Äôt have the bandwidth or skills to do everything that‚Äôs required to build a scalable startup by themselves. Of course, one can always outsource part of the work or hire more employees, but that means time and money, which often isn‚Äôt viable in the early stages. If you're looking for the quickest win with the highest ROI, form a great co-founding team.

On the contrary, having the wrong co-founding team, is amongst the top reasons why companies fail ‚Äî which usually comes down to premature pairing and a lack of understanding of who the other person really is and what's important to them. This means it‚Äôs crucial to think through the type of person(s) you want to work with, how you will test working with them, and how to evaluate your options objectively.

Although there‚Äôs no cookie-cutter approach to finding co-founders, below are some ways that have led to successful co-founder matching at Antler:

üëâ Chemistry-based match: This is when founders team up based on great social fit and develop ideas together. Just be aware ‚Äî founders in this scenario can quickly end up with a co-founder who‚Äôs very similar to them (also called ‚ÄúAffinity Bias‚Äù, which describes our preference to spend time with those who are similar to us in looks, interests, beliefs, etc). While this might feel fun and comfortable, it also often means less diverse and creative views, which is crucial to startup success. 

üëâ Founder on a mission: This is when a founder with a pre-existing idea recruits a team of co-founders, each with complementary skills. For example, a domain expert partnered with a tech wizard and a growth legend would undoubtedly form a strong team.

üëâ Common passion: This is when founders take part in vertical-specific sprints and bond over common interests to disrupt an industry they‚Äôre passionate about. You may not have in-depth prior knowledge about the industry, but having the willingness to become obsessed with an area and a co-founder who‚Äôs a domain expert, far outweighs that need.

üëâ Develop an idea in Antler, find co-founders from external network: This is when a founder develops a sound idea with a proven market fit but fails to find a co-founder within the cohort. In this scenario, Antler works with the individual to bring in an external founder who is a good fit for the company.

üí° Keep in mind: a complementary skillset and personality is definitely not the only thing you should be looking for. You also need to make sure that your vision and values are aligned, and that there‚Äôs chemistry between you üëá
1Ô∏è‚É£ Chemistry - an absolute must in a co-founding team. Founders need to be able to see themselves working together for the next 3-10 years. Learn more about the first-hand experience relating chemistry from one of the Antler founders in Sydney.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Shared values - how will you run the company? It's critical that you agree on a suitable work style such as office hours, physical/remote working, how to give/receive feedback, but also financial incentives such as how the equity should be split.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Same vision - it is important to have a very aligned view on where to take the company and find clear ways to align on differences. Even though at the beginning it might be difficult to see an exact vision, it is crucial to talk about it from the very start.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Complementary skillsets - it‚Äôs good to have different strengths, e.g. the classic tech/business split. But there are multiple ways to think about this. It could also be two techies, one with a front-end spike and one with an infrastructure spike.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Complementary personalities - a good team consists of team members who have different personality strengths. Consider comparing your MBTI test results!
FINDING CO-FOUNDER(S): HOW TO START

üëâ Start with yourself


First of all, find your superpowers - things you are good at and keen on doing. Then, be brutally honest with yourself and confront your weaknesses. When you identify your strengths and weak points, look for an individual who completes you. The ideal co-founder will possess the skills that you're lacking. 
 

üëâ The 3H concept


A popular concept introduced by several notable investors and founders is that an efficient founding team consists of three types of people: a Hacker, a Hipster, and a Hustler (‚ÄúThe 3H‚Äù). The Hacker builds the product or service, The Hipster designs it, and the Hustler sells it.

The 3H concept is based on the belief that complementary skillsets in the founding team are critical to effectively build and scale a company. However, it does not mean that you need three people in every co-founding team. For example, some people can be both be a great ‚ÄúHustler‚Äù and ‚ÄúHipster‚Äù, and others can be a fantastic CTO while also having tremendous sales capabilities.
 

üëâ Get started today


As a founder, you will need to know exactly who you are, what you want to achieve, as well as your own personal motivations and vision. Knowing your why will help you build your value foundations, maintain self-alignment through the program and create direction in your company.

Check out our self-reflection questions! (please make a local copy before you start filling it out)

Skills & Value matching 
Write down a clear description of your ideal co-founder. What are the key roles and responsibilities you want your co-founder to be doing?  What questions will you use to test if your co-founder meets your criteria? If you are looking for a technical co-founder, how will you evaluate their skills? What values are the most critical? Communicate, get through the tough questions early, and be prepared to move fast based on your intuition.
 

üëâ Be transparent in your communication


If you meet someone with whom you see yourself working with, that‚Äôs fantastic!

But don‚Äôt worry, it‚Äôs okay to tell them before you start working together, that you want to meet other people and have more in-depth discussions relating your ideas, before you track-out as co-founders. Remember to be open and transparent in your communication. It‚Äôs clear that you want to exploit all the opportunities ‚Äî the same goes for everyone.

Just remember that giving a clear heads-up to your potential co-founder is crucial to have a strong and honest foundation of your relationship.
